===================
RENT AND PAYMENT TERMS
===================

Monthly Rent: $2,400.00
Due Date: 1st day of each month
Late Fee: $75.00 if rent is not received by the 5th day of the month
Payment Methods: 
- Online portal (preferred)
- Check payable to Metropolitan Property Management LLC
- Bank transfer to account provided

Rent Increases:
Landlord may increase rent with 60 days written notice. Rent increases are limited to 5% per year in accordance with local regulations.


===================
SECURITY DEPOSIT
===================

Amount: $4,800.00 (equivalent to 2 months rent)
Due: Before move-in date
Held: In separate escrow account at First Demo Bank

Conditions for Return:
- Property returned in original condition (normal wear and tear excepted)
- All rent and utilities paid in full
- No damage beyond normal use
- Professional cleaning completed
- 14-day written notice provided before move-out

Deposit Return Timeline:
Landlord will inspect property within 7 days of move-out and return deposit (less any valid deductions) within 30 days, along with an itemized statement of any deductions.


===================
TERMINATION NOTICE PERIOD
===================

Tenant Termination:
- During lease term: 60 days written notice required
- If breaking lease early, tenant responsible for rent until new tenant found or lease end date (whichever comes first)
- Early termination fee: 2 months rent if notice is less than 60 days
- Notice must be submitted via certified mail or email with read receipt

Landlord Termination:
- During lease term: Only for cause (non-payment, lease violation, property sale)
- Month-to-month tenancy: 90 days written notice required
- Property sale: 120 days notice required to allow tenant first right of refusal

Notice Requirements:
All notices must be in writing and delivered via:
- Certified mail with return receipt
- Email with read receipt confirmation
- Hand delivery with signed acknowledgment


===================
PROPERTY INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
===================

LANDLORD'S INSURANCE:
Landlord maintains property insurance covering:
- Building structure and common areas
- Liability for property-related incidents
- Fire and natural disaster coverage

FLOOD INSURANCE:
The property is located in a FEMA-designated flood zone X (minimal flood risk). Landlord maintains flood insurance coverage for the building structure as required by the mortgage lender. This policy covers structural damage to the building but DOES NOT cover tenant's personal belongings in case of flood damage.

TENANT'S INSURANCE (REQUIRED):
Tenant MUST obtain and maintain renter's insurance throughout the lease term with minimum coverage:
- Personal property: $30,000
- Liability: $100,000
- Loss of use: $10,000
- Flood coverage for personal property: STRONGLY RECOMMENDED but not required

Proof of insurance must be provided to landlord:
- Within 14 days of lease signing
- Upon each policy renewal
- Insurance must name landlord as additional interested party

Failure to maintain insurance:
First violation: Written warning
Second violation: $100 per month fee until insurance obtained
Continued violation: Grounds for lease termination

- Structural repairs (roof, walls, foundation)
- HVAC system maintenance and repairs
- Plumbing and electrical system repairs
- Appliance repairs (provided appliances only)
- Common area maintenance
- Pest control (unless caused by tenant negligence)
- Exterior painting and maintenance
- Snow removal from common areas and walkways

Landlord Emergency Contact: (555) 123-4567 (24/7)
Non-Emergency Repairs: Submit via online portal

Response Times:
- Emergency repairs (no heat, water, gas leak): Within 4 hours
- Urgent repairs (broken appliance, plumbing issues): Within 48 hours
- Non-urgent repairs: Within 7 business days

TENANT RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Light bulb replacement
- Air filter changes (monthly)
- Drain clogs (unless caused by structural issue)
- Minor wall repairs and touch-up painting
- Cleaning gutters (if applicable)
- Lawn care and snow removal from assigned areas
- Damage caused by tenant or guests
- Pest control if infestation caused by tenant negligence

Tenant Maintenance Obligations:
- Report all maintenance issues within 48 hours of discovery
- Provide access for repairs during reasonable hours (24 hours notice required except emergencies)
- Prevent damage from occurring due to negligence

===================

- Property inspections: 7 days written notice (quarterly maximum)

===================
LEASE VIOLATIONS AND REMEDIES
===================

Common Violations:
- Late rent payment: Late fee applied
- Unauthorized pets: $200 fine per incident, pet must be removed
- Noise violations: Written warning, then $100 fine per violation
- Smoking inside: $500 fine, repeat violations lead to termination
- Property damage: Repair costs charged to tenant

Landlord Remedies:
- First violation: Written warning
- Second violation: Monetary fine as specified
- Third violation: Notice to cure or quit (14 days)
- Continued violations: Lease termination and eviction proceedings

Tenant Remedies:
- Landlord failure to maintain: Written notice with 14 days to cure
- Uninhabitable conditions: Right to withhold rent (must follow legal procedures)
- Illegal entry: Tenant may change locks and seek damages
